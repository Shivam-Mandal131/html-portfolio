Welcome to the Taj Mahal

The Symbol of Love

The Taj Mahal is a breathtaking masterpiece of Mughal architecture, located in the city of Agra, India. Built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century, this stunning white marble mausoleum is a symbol of eternal love and devotion. The monument was created in memory of Shah Jahan's beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who passed away during childbirth. The construction of the Taj Mahal took over 20 years and involved the labor of over 20,000 workers. The monument's intricate carvings, stunning domes, and perfect symmetry are a testament to the exceptional craftsmanship of the Mughal artisans.

The Taj Mahal's exterior is made of white marble, which was brought from far-off quarries in Rajasthan. The marble is inlaid with precious stones like jasper and jade, which add to the monument's grandeur. The building's interior is equally impressive, with a central chamber that houses the tombs of Shah Jahan and Mumtaz Mahal. The tomb of Mumtaz Mahal is placed in the center and is surrounded by four smaller tombs symbolizing the couple's children.

The Taj Mahal is surrounded by lush gardens that enhance its beauty .The gardens are divided into four parts by water channels and walkways, creating a perfect symmetry that complements the monument's design. The gardens are adorned with fountains, pools, and trees, making it an ideal place for visitors to relax and take in the monument's grandeur. The Taj Mahal is an iconic symbol of India's rich cultural heritage and has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It attracts millions of visitors every year who come to marvel at its beauty and history. The monument's timeless beauty and exceptional craftsmanship continue to inspire people worldwide, making it one of the most famous and visited tourist attractions in the world.


Nearby attractions
Agra Fort
Also known as Lal Qila, Fort Rouge or Red Fort of Agra, the Agra Fort is a UNESCO world heritage site. It is situated at a distance of about 2.5 km northwest of the famous Taj Mahal.

Fatehpur Sikri
A city, predominantly made of red sand stone, Fatehpur Sikri was once the capital of Mughal Emperor Akbar. It is now a UNESCO world heritage site and a famous tourist attraction.
 Itimad-ud-daula's Tomb
This tomb nestled on the bank of the river Yamuna was built by Queen Noor Jahan. It was constructed using yellow, black and white marble inlay, and was hence called the Baby Taj.

Akbar's Tomb
Akbar's tomb is the tomb of the Mughal emperor, Akbar and an important Mughal architectural masterpiece. It was built in 1604-1613 and is situated in 119 acres of grounds.
